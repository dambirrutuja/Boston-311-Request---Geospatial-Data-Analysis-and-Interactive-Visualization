Key Insights

Six data-driven findings with direct operational implications for Boston city services.

📍 Geographic Concentration

Dorchester, Roxbury, and the South End generate the highest complaint volumes. Resource allocation should be weighted toward these areas during peak periods.

☀️ Seasonal Surge

Summer months see ~30% more complaints than the annual average. Proactive staffing adjustments during June–August would improve response times significantly.

⏰ Long-Tail Resolution Problem

While most cases close in under 3 days, the top 10% take over 3 weeks. Targeted intervention for cases approaching 14 days outstanding would reduce this tail.

⚖️ Neighborhood Equity Gap

Some neighborhoods wait 5–8× longer for the same service type as others. A neighborhood equity dashboard would enable transparent, accountable tracking.

📋 SLA Non-Compliance Hotspots

Certain departments consistently miss SLA targets. Workflow analysis within those departments is recommended to identify and address bottlenecks.

🗺️ Cluster-Based Routing Opportunity

5 K-Means geographic zones align well with neighborhood bands. Zone-based routing rather than citywide dispatch could reduce response overhead by 15–20%.
